ABOUT

In light of the siege that the Gaza Strip suffers from and the consequent interruption of face-to-face communication with the outside world, and the interruption of the cultural and artistic side’s access to the other side, from here the idea came to launch a commercial and cultural website on the Internet that revolves around the artistic cultural aspect in the Gaza Strip, It includes a number of artists in several fields to spread their talents such as drawing, photography, fashion design and embroideries to sell their paintings and works and spreads culture outside the country and Due to the limited local exhibitions in the Gaza Strip and the institutions that support the artistic cultural aspect due to many factors, the most important of which is the recent siege on the Gaza Strip and the limited artistic awareness of the groups of society as a whole. And because art today is one of the most powerful peaceful weapons that are used to spread cultures between countries, which Gaza lacks despite the need, we 8 had to work in a site that collects local artworks and presents them to other communities in the process of displaying Palestinian art well and stabilizing and unifying Palestinian artworks with all Its forms and times and will Facilitate linking people with limited talents in the artistic field with external institutions to support them and embrace them with job opportunities and their participation in external exhibitions and help them sell their paintings and artworks of various forms and times outside the country

1- Painting

Drawing is an important expressionist tool that can be used to express the various ideas that are in the human mind, making it has a great influence on everyone who sees and interact with it visually. May sometimes be an important expression of some of the events that occurred during the period in which the painter lived, the painting can show the cases of victory, bloody, determination, failure, treachery, abandonment, and all human historical situations. Is considered an embodiment of beauty, where this art is one of the best arts that can express beauty in an attractive and beautiful way, using fonts and colors and many other tools, especially imagination and thought. Drawing increases creativity, activates the brain and helps people to have a beautiful mind. It is considered a means of resistance against the occupiers, as happens in Palestine, where exhibitions are held here and there in order to make the world know the crimes of the occupation army.

3- Photography

Photography is one of the arts that embodies the beauty of the moment in which we live at its best, and while it does not need a lot of skills, it does require an understanding in order to take great pictures, such as understanding a number of photography concepts such as lighting techniques, backgrounds, arrangements and so on, so when the picture is finished, the result will be a great shot. Practicing photography is another way to perfect your shots. The more practice you get, the better your shots will be. Short courses can be taken to learn in detail to understand how to take photos so that they look natural and great. Photography develops the imaginative senses and the level of patience of those who practice it, as it waits a long time in order to take a great and correct picture, and a good photographer will always look for good scenes or events to capture striking pictures, especially on happy occasions, I can say that I just love to do photography, Whether you’re in the middle of landscapes or indoors amid walls. 4- Embroidery

Once a craft practiced by village women, "tatreez" has become a symbol of women's resistance, identity and heritage. The society or societies concerned with this embroidery : 1- All Palestinians wherever they live, as embroidery is intertwined in many popular practices and rituals and it is an integral part of their wear, that is taught to daughters by their grandmothers and mothers. 2- Women and girls in Palestinian society as a whole, in cities, villages, and refugee camps, and wherever Palestinian women live in Palestine or in their places of refuge. 3- Institutions supporting the embroidery heritage done by male and female employees working in the workshops of charitable societies. 4- Factories’ and workshops’ owners who use embroidery units in their products such as workshops making accessories and traditional jewelry. 5- Popular artists interested in heritage such as the Dabkeh groups, as well as painters who express their community in their.
